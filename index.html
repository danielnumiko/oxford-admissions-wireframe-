The DPhil in Engineering Science is the largest in the UK, and one of the largest in the world. We have more than 75 full faculty permanent academics, and around 450 graduate students. The DPhil (PhD) is a research degree that allows you to pursue in-depth investigation in your chosen field under the guidance of world-class academics.

About the course

Engineering is an intellectually complex and dynamic subject, from the global network of microchips enabling the internet, through autonomous research-based algorithms, to the science, engineering and economics of sustainable energy. The art of the engineer is to produce a coherent system. This requires appreciation of context, analysis to achieve understanding, then synthesis and technical creativity to achieve innovative, plausible and ultimately desirable outcomes.

The DPhil encompasses all areas of engineering from civil through power, transport, chemical and biomedical engineering to complex systems. It is a research degree where you will carry out an original piece of research, culminating in a thesis. Many of our students work in industrial settings and develop research-based solution to real technical problems.

Entry requirements (2025-26) ▼

Academic Requirements

A first-class or strong upper second-class undergraduate degree with honours (or equivalent international qualifications) is required as a minimum, in engineering or a related discipline relevant for the proposed area of research, such as physics, materials science, computer science, applied mathematics or chemistry.

English Language Requirements

If your first language is not English, you may need to provide evidence that you meet the University's higher level English language requirement.

Prior Research Experience

Previous research experience is highly valued. You should demonstrate a clear understanding of your chosen research area and its importance to the field.

Contacting Supervisors

It is recommended that you contact a potential supervisor ahead of submitting your application. You should research the specialisms of the department and those of your potential supervisor before making contact.

Additional Requirements

No Graduate Record Examination (GRE) or GMAT scores are required for this programme.

Before You Apply

Before submitting your application:

  • Research the department's specialisms and identify your research interests
  • Contact potential supervisors to discuss your research proposal
  • Ensure you meet the entry requirements
  • Prepare your supporting documents including CV, research proposal, and references

Application Routes

  • Three academic references
  • CV/Resume
  • English language evidence (if applicable)

After submission, your application will be reviewed by the department. Shortlisted candidates may be invited for an interview. Successful applicants will receive offers typically between January and April for October entry.

About the course

The DPhil in Population Health provides training for students to pursue research that addresses major population-level health challenges. Students join one of the largest concentrations of population health researchers in the world, conducting high-quality epidemiological studies, randomised trials, and developing novel statistical and computational methods.

Research areas include cardiovascular epidemiology, cancer epidemiology, health informatics, clinical trials, genetic epidemiology, statistical methods, and global health. Our students work with large-scale datasets including UK Biobank, the China Kadoorie Biobank, and numerous randomised trials.

Ruskin School of Art

The Ruskin School of Art is one of the leading centres for contemporary fine art practice and research in Europe. Our graduate programmes combine studio practice with critical and theoretical study.

Graduate Courses

MFA in Fine Art

A two-year studio-based Master of Fine Arts programme that provides an intensive and supportive environment for the development of individual artistic practice.

Duration: 2 years | Mode: Full-time

DPhil in Fine Art

A research degree combining studio practice with written thesis. Candidates develop substantial body of work alongside critical and theoretical research.

Duration: 3-4 years | Mode: Full-time / Part-time

MSt in History of Art and Visual Culture

An intensive taught Masters programme exploring historical and contemporary approaches to art history and visual culture studies.

Duration: 1 year | Mode: Full-time
