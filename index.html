DPhil in Engineering Science

The DPhil in Engineering Science is the largest in the UK, and one of the largest in the world. We have more than 75 full faculty permanent academics, and around 450 graduate students. The DPhil (PhD) is a research degree that allows you to pursue in-depth investigation in your chosen field under the guidance of world-class academics.

About the course

Engineering is an intellectually complex and dynamic subject, from the global network of microchips enabling the internet, through autonomous research-based algorithms, to the science, engineering and economics of sustainable energy. The art of the engineer is to produce a coherent system. This requires appreciation of context, analysis to achieve understanding, then synthesis and technical creativity to achieve innovative, plausible and ultimately desirable outcomes.

The DPhil encompasses all areas of engineering from civil through power, transport, chemical and biomedical engineering to complex systems. It is a research degree where you will carry out an original piece of research, culminating in a thesis. Many of our students work in industrial settings and develop research-based solution to real technical problems.

Machine Learning for Autonomous Systems

Supervisor: Prof. Jane Smith

This project explores novel deep learning architectures for real-time decision making in autonomous vehicles. Focus on safety-critical systems and robust perception under uncertainty.

STUDENTSHIP AVAILABLE

Full Description:

The project will investigate state-of-the-art neural network architectures for autonomous vehicle navigation, with particular emphasis on safety and robustness. You will develop novel algorithms that can handle uncertain sensor data and make reliable decisions in complex urban environments. The research will combine theoretical advances in machine learning with practical implementation on physical robot platforms.

Required Background: Strong programming skills (Python, C++), knowledge of machine learning fundamentals, experience with robotics or computer vision preferred.

Quantum Computing for Optimization Problems

Supervisor: Dr. Michael Chen

Research into quantum algorithms for solving large-scale optimization problems in logistics and supply chain management. Collaboration with industry partners.

Full Description:

This project aims to develop practical quantum algorithms that can outperform classical methods for combinatorial optimization. You will work on both theoretical algorithm design and experimental implementation using quantum computing platforms. The project includes collaboration with logistics companies to test algorithms on real-world problems.

Required Background: Strong mathematical foundation, quantum mechanics knowledge, programming experience, interest in practical applications.

Sustainable Materials for Energy Storage

Supervisor: Prof. Sarah Williams

Development of novel electrode materials using sustainable, earth-abundant elements for next-generation battery technologies. Focus on improving energy density and cycle life.

STUDENTSHIP AVAILABLE

Full Description:

The research will focus on designing and synthesizing new battery electrode materials that avoid rare earth elements. You will use a combination of computational modeling and experimental synthesis to identify promising candidates, then characterize their electrochemical performance. The goal is to develop materials that can enable affordable, sustainable energy storage for grid applications.

Required Background: Chemistry or materials science background, laboratory experience, knowledge of electrochemistry preferred.

DPhil in Population Health

The DPhil in Population Health is one of the largest in the UK, and one of the largest in the world. The Nuffield Department of Population Health (NDPH) is dedicated to conducting research that addresses major population-level health challenges through high-quality epidemiological studies, randomised trials, and novel statistical methods.

About the course

The DPhil in Population Health provides training for students to pursue research that addresses major population-level health challenges. Students join one of the largest concentrations of population health researchers in the world, conducting high-quality epidemiological studies, randomised trials, and developing novel statistical and computational methods.

Research areas include cardiovascular epidemiology, cancer epidemiology, health informatics, clinical trials, genetic epidemiology, statistical methods, and global health. Our students work with large-scale datasets including UK Biobank, the China Kadoorie Biobank, and numerous randomised trials.
